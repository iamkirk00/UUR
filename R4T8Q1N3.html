How We Built It

  • Measured & Cut: The frame needed lots of cuts on the PVC pipe. We measured twice and cut once to avoid wrong lengths that could make the robot look funny and not work.
  • Drilled Holes: We drilled holes in the PVC and motor canisters, using spare wood to protect the table.
  • Soldering: We melted metal at 350 degrees onto the controller and wire terminals. A mistake could’ve caused severe burns.
  • Wax Sealed: We sealed motors with wax in canisters to waterproof them. A mistake here would stop the motor from moving.
  • Testing: We tested the robot with pool noodles and hooks. Without testing, the propeller could’ve come off, and it wouldn’t work.

What We Built

  • Frame: Made of PVC pipes to hold everything together.
  • Motors: A case and base spin the propeller to move the frame.
  • Controller: A base with switches and wires to activate motors.
  • Wiring: Metal inside rubber or plastic moves power from controller to motor.
  • Hooks: Curved metal pieces pick up rings and carry them to the surface.
  • Cut Pool Noodles: Keep the rover buoyant so it doesn’t sink too fast.

What Did We Learn

  • Importance of a Fuse: A motor can pop a fuse if it’s not good enough. Fuses in houses and cars stop electricity when something goes wrong.
  • Being Careful: We measured PVC twice before cutting and were cautious with the hot soldering iron.
  • Electrical Currents: We learned positive (colored) and negative (black) sides of wires for electrical flow.
